What Are Cookies

def what_are_cookies():

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work correctly, to remember your preferences, and to give website owners information about how their site is being used.

Code Sense (accessible at pansensoyglenn-dev.github.io/code-sense/) uses cookies and similar technologies such as local storage and session storage. This policy covers all of them.

# principle

I apply data minimisation: we use only the cookies that are genuinely necessary or that you have explicitly opted into. We do not use advertising networks, cross-site tracking pixels, or behavioural profiling.

05

Third-Party Cookies

def third_party_cookies():

Code Sense limits third-party cookies to an absolute minimum. The only third-party cookie we may set — and only with your explicit consent — is the LinkedIn Insight Tag for measuring the effectiveness of any LinkedIn-based promotions.

I do not embed Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, TikTok Pixel, or any other behavioural advertising or cross-site tracking technology. Our analytics are handled via a self-hosted, privacy-first instance of Plausible Analytics, which does not use cookies at all for its core measurement.

✓ commitment

I will never add a third-party tracking or advertising cookie without updating this policy and obtaining fresh consent from you first.

06

Browser Controls

def browser_controls():

In addition to the preference panel above, you can manage cookies directly in your browser. Most browsers allow you to view, block, or delete cookies at any time:

Chrome: Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data.

Firefox: Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data.

Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data.

Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data.

⚠ note

Blocking essential cookies may cause parts of Code Sense to stop working correctly. If you experience issues after disabling cookies, please contact us.

07

Do Not Track

def do_not_track():

Some browsers can send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. Because there is currently no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, Code Sense does not alter its behaviour in response to them.

However, our analytics implementation already does not track individuals across sites, does not create user profiles, and does not share data with advertising networks — regardless of DNT. We consider our baseline privacy posture to be in the spirit of DNT by default.
