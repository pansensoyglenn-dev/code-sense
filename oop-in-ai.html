🤖 THE AI AGENT AS AN OBJECT
Modern AI agents are designed as objects with private state (memory), public interfaces (capabilities), and encapsulated complexity. This pattern enables autonomous operation while maintaining control and observability. 
class AutonomousAgent:
    def __init__(self, agent_id: str, model: str):
        self.__agent_id = agent_id
        self.__memory = []
        self.__tools = {}
        self._status = "idle"
    
    def perceive(self, input_data: dict) -> None:
        self.__memory.append(input_data)
        self._reason()
    
    def _reason(self) -> None:
        context = self.__compress_memory()
        self._plan(context)
    
    def act(self) -> dict:
        return self.__tools.execute_plan()

💡 WHY OOP MATTERS MORE THAN EVER

In 2026, OOP is not being replaced—it is being elevated. As one analysis puts it: "The core ideas that traveled through decades—encapsulation, inheritance, polymorphism, abstraction—remain the bedrock of robust AI systems." AI excels at generating function-level code, but architectural design, boundary definition, and maintaining invariants remain deeply human responsibilities. OOP provides the vocabulary for humans to collaborate with AI, the structure for agents to interoperate, and the discipline for systems to scale safely.

The most significant shift is cognitive: from writing every line to designing the objects that will be instantiated, configured, and even negotiated by AI. The developer's role is becoming that of an architect and conductor—shaping the agents, their capabilities, and their protocols. In this world, a deep understanding of OOP is not legacy knowledge; it is the core competence for building the intelligent systems of the future.
